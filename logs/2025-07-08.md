# Survivor Log - Day 35

**Rainy Reflections at Fort Crimson**

The constant drumming of rain against the bunker's metal roof kept me awake most of the night. That and the occasional scraping sounds from the compound outside - whether it was the armored undead or just debris in the wind, I couldn't tell. The fungal Geiger counter kept pulsing softly, its readings distorted by the weather patterns. I swear I could feel the vibrations through my chitinous growths, like the whole base was breathing.

Spent the night trying to improve our water filtration system with the last scraps of metal we had. The pressure cooker helped distill some rainwater, but without proper materials, it was like trying to build a dam with toothpicks. The work kept my hands busy though, kept me from thinking about how our food supplies hit zero yesterday.

At dawn, I noticed something strange - the fungal map I'd been working on had developed new markings overnight. Three zones pulsed with a faint bioluminescence where the map got wet from a leak in the ceiling. Safe zones? Or fungal hotspots? Hard to tell the difference anymore. The rain seems to be accelerating whatever transformation is happening to me - my carapace itches constantly now, like it's growing new layers.

The underground tunnels here still smell of gunpowder and rotting meat. I can hear the military zombies moving through them, their armor clanking with that distinctive metallic echo. Tomorrow I'll have to risk venturing toward the armory - we need weapons more than ever with that storm coming. The radio picked up another SOS transmission, same as yesterday, but the static from the approaching storm made it impossible to triangulate.

I keep thinking about the Sporebound group we met at the flooded passage. They seemed to understand what's happening to me better than I do. Maybe after this storm passes...
