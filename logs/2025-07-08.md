# Survivor Log - Day 33

**Location:** The Forgotten Tunnels - Near the Fungal Grove  
**Weather:** The rain's steady drumming echoes through the tunnels like a ghostly heartbeat. Water drips through cracks in the ceiling, forming small pools that reflect the flickering emergency lights. The air smells of wet concrete and something... else. Something alive.

---

I spent the night scavenging near the Glass Canopy, where the fungal growths glow faintly in the dark. The rain made everything slippery, but it also masked the sound of my movements. My fungal instincts tingled as I worked - I could feel vibrations through the growths, warning me when shamblers were near.

Found something miraculous in an abandoned medic's bag tucked under a train seat - **antibiotics**. Actual, unexpired antibiotics. My fungal rash has been getting worse, and these might be my ticket to not ending up as mushroom food. The bag also contained some sterile bandages, which I added to my dwindling medical supplies.

The Sporebound left another message near the Flooded Passage - crude symbols scratched into the concrete that my fungal map helped decipher. They're warning about increased Fog Reaper activity near the Transit Hub. I saw evidence of their traps too - strange fungal pods hanging from the ceiling that release spores when disturbed.

That Morse code transmission keeps looping. "... --- ...". SOS. But from where? My radio picks up nothing but static between the raindrops. Maybe the storm tomorrow will clear the interference.

My body aches from the chitinous growths hardening across my skin. They saved me from a shambler's bite last week, but now I can barely turn my head. Need to find a way to slow this mutation before I become a human beetle.

---

**Tomorrow's Priorities:**  
1. Use the antibiotics before infection sets in  
2. Investigate that SOS signal  
3. Avoid the Transit Hub - Fog Reapers are hunting  
4. Find medical supplies to treat this damn carapace  

The tunnels whisper to me more each day. Sometimes I think they're trying to help.
