# Survivor Log - Day 32

**Location:** Central Station  
**Weather:** The rain hasn't let up since yesterday. That steady drumming against the broken glass canopy above creates an eerie white noise that both comforts and unnerves me. The fungal growths seem to pulse brighter in the damp air, their bioluminescence casting shifting shadows on the tunnel walls.

---

Last night I tried signaling again from the generator room, hoping someone would answer the repeated SOS I've been picking up. The rain made transmission difficult - every crackle of static made me jump, imagining it was the hive stirring beneath my feet. But nothing came through except that same looping Morse code, fainter than before.

The fungal rash on my arms has spread despite my attempts to treat it. I swear the patterns look almost intentional now, like some kind of living map. My new fungal Geiger counter keeps clicking near certain patches of growth, its readings correlating with the pulsing pain in my skin. 

I found something disturbing near the Flooded Passage - a deep chasm I'm calling the Fungal Chasm. The walls were... breathing. Not just the air currents making the spores swirl, but actual rhythmic expansion and contraction. When I threw a glow stick down, the light revealed fungal structures that looked disturbingly like nerve clusters. The Sporebound would probably worship this place.

That's when I saw them - a cloaked figure watching from the old control room balcony. By the time I waded through the knee-deep water, they'd vanished, leaving behind a crude map scratched into the concrete with what might have been fingernails. It showed three locations marked with what my fungal instincts tell me are "safe zones" - places the hive avoids.

The rain is both a blessing and curse today. It masks my movements but also makes the fungi more active. I can hear them creaking and popping as they expand in the moisture. That serrated knife I took from the Fog Reapers' trap seems to cut through them easier than normal blades. Maybe I'll try harvesting some - this "Fungal Forager" instinct keeps suggesting certain growths might have medicinal properties.

Tomorrow I need to investigate those marked locations. But first, I need to treat this rash before it becomes something worse. The sterile bandages barely stick to my damp skin.

---
