# Survivor Log - Day 34

**Location:** Central Station  
**Weather:** The rain hasn't let up since yesterday. That steady drumming against the subway grates above has become both a blessing and a curse - it masks my movements but also makes it impossible to hear approaching threats. The air smells like wet concrete and something else... that fungal stench is getting stronger.  

Last night I risked signaling again from the broken glass canopy. The flickering light must have carried far in this weather because this morning I found something terrifying - a fresh Watcher symbol carved into the station wall near my sleeping area. That triangle with the eye inside was still oozing some kind of blue fungal sap. They were here while I slept.  

The fungal Geiger counter picked up something new too - intermittent pulses coming from the direction of the Flooded Passage. Not the usual hive vibrations, but something more... organized. Like a heartbeat.  

My rash has gotten worse with the dampness. The antibiotics helped, but I can feel new spores trying to take root in my skin. The chitinous patches itch constantly.  

**New Discovery:** While checking the perimeter, I nearly fell through a collapsed section near the Transit Hub. Beneath the rubble was what looks like an old bio-lab entrance. The stench hit me first - rotting flesh mixed with harsh chemicals. Through the broken containment glass I could see... things moving in the shadows. Whatever happened here, it wasn't just the apocalypse. This was ground zero for something much worse.  

**Plan for Today:**  
1. Treat fungal rash with remaining antibiotics  
2. Scout the bio-lab entrance (carefully)  
3. Monitor the strange pulsing from Flooded Passage  
4. Boil rainwater using the pressure cooker  

The Watchers' mark means they're watching me now. I need to decide - make contact or disappear deeper into the tunnels before the coming storm hits.
