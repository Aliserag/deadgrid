# Survivor Log - Day 30

**Location:** Central Station  
**Weather:** Howling Storm (Severity 5/5)  

The storm outside is making the tunnels sing. Not the usual fungal whispers - this is something deeper, like the whole subway system has become a giant wind instrument. The emergency lights keep flickering in time with distant thunder, casting strobe-like shadows that make me jump at every movement.  

Last night was spent reinforcing our position. Boarded up the last weak points in the station's western entrance where the fungal growths were getting too close. Found strange markings on the walls - not Watcher symbols, but something organic, like the fungi itself was trying to communicate. My Geiger counter went wild near the collapsed tunnel - the hive is definitely digging closer.  

Found a group calling themselves the Sporebound near the Flooded Passage. They move through the fungal groves like ghosts, their skin covered in bioluminescent spores. Offered to trade knowledge of fungal purification techniques for our spare medical supplies. Had to refuse - their eyes had that glassy look of the infected, but... not quite.  

The storm's making everything worse:  
- Water's rising in the lower tunnels (that Flooded Passage is now completely submerged)  
- Fungal pulses come with every lightning strike  
- Can hear the Iron Vanguard's traps triggering in the distance  
- Our makeshift barricades creak under the pressure  

But there's opportunity too - the heavy rain means our water collectors are overflowing. Used the pressure cooker to sterilize enough for three days. That's one problem solved.  

Tomorrow I need to:  
1. Check the generator before fuel runs out  
2. Investigate those new digging sounds near the Transit Hub  
3. Maybe reconsider the Sporebound's offer...  

The whispers keep saying "soon." I don't know if that's the storm, the hive, or my own fractured mind.
