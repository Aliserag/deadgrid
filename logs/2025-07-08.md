# Survivor Log - Day 25

The flickering emergency lights cast long shadows across the damp concrete as I worked through the night. That strange fungal sensitivity makes my skin crawl every time the spores drift through the air, but at least it helps me notice things others might miss. 

Last night I risked signaling from the Supply Cache area, hoping to trace that fungal whisper distress call. The cloudy spring weather made it harder - the thick blanket of clouds diffused my flashlight beams and muffled sounds in strange ways. Twice I thought I heard answering clicks from the ventilation shafts, but it might have just been dripping water echoing through the tunnels. 

Nothing came of it. Just more unanswered static and that ever-present fungal pulsing in the deeper tunnels. The Watchers left another warning near the Generator Room - crude symbols scratched into the concrete that seem to say "light attracts teeth." I don't know whether they mean the emergency lights or my signals. 

Found some useful supplies though:
- 2 medical kits in an abandoned train car (that fungal sensitivity paid off)
- A working Geiger counter fused with fungal growths (might help track the source of those whispers)
- Extra batteries from a maintenance locker

The Iron Vanguard traps near the Transit Hub are getting more sophisticated. Saw one poor soul's remains tangled in razor wire and fungal tendrils. Need to be careful moving through there. 

Morale's low. These cloudy days make it impossible to tell if it's dawn or dusk, and the fungal whispers never stop. But I'm learning to read the growth patterns now - there's meaning in the pulsing rhythms if you watch long enough. Maybe tomorrow I'll follow the newest fungal trail markers toward Echo Ridge Estates. The Fog Dwellers seem to be leading somewhere...
